Transformation family:
PREVENTING_CONDITION_ACTION_TRANSFORMATION

Family goal:
Rewrite the story so that the first main action creates, implies, or establishes a condition that prevents the second main action from being carried out.

Family-specific constraints:
- The blocking relation should arise from a system state, object state, session state, validation outcome, or restriction that makes the second action not possible as described.
- The blocking relation should make the second action not possible as described, not merely delayed, discouraged, or less convenient.
- The first action should function as a genuine preventing condition for the second action, not merely as something earlier, different, inconvenient, or loosely related.
- Natural blocking markers are allowed when they fit the story, but do not rely mainly on one narrow repeated phrase pattern such as "cannot", "unless", or "until" across most examples.
- Do not turn the rewrite into a simple paraphrase, a prerequisite/enabling case, or a loosely related two-action story.
- Prefer blocking cases built around concrete action verbs such as lock, cancel, revoke, disable, suspend, reject, archive, invalidate, or deactivate.
- Avoid examples that merely express workflow order or prerequisites, since these are more likely to be interpreted as enabling rather than blocking.

  }
