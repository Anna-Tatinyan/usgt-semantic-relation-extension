Transformation family:
PRECONDITION_ACTION_TRANSFORMATION

Family goal:
Rewrite the story so that the first main action establishes a condition, state, verification, setup, or resource needed for the second main action to be carried out.

Family-specific constraints:
- The first action should function as a reasonably necessary precondition for the second, not merely as something helpful or loosely related.
- The enabling effect should arise from the story content, not only from one repeated phrase pattern.
- Natural precondition markers are allowed when they fit the story, but do not rely mainly on one narrow repeated phrase pattern such as "before I can", "so that I can", "in order to", or "after verifying" across most examples.
- Do not turn the rewrite into a blocking case or a simple paraphrase of the source.
- Prefer enabling cases built around concrete action verbs such as verify, authenticate, configure, connect, submit, approve, or register.
- Avoid examples in which the enabling relation depends mainly on weak benefit-clause verbs or loosely attached subordinate verbs.

Example:

[
  {
    "story_id": "1",
    "source_story": "As a user, I want to update my profile so that my information stays current."
  }
]
