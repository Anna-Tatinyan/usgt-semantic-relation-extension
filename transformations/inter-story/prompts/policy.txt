Transformation family:
POLICY_TRANSFORMATION

Family goal:
Rewrite the source story by changing one central permission rule, ownership rule, approval authority, or access-control rule so that the source story and rewritten story cannot both be satisfied as stated, while remaining in the same feature area.

Family-specific constraints:
- Preserve the same feature topic and overlapping subject matter.
- Keep the same action, object, and feature intent wherever possible.
- The incompatibility must arise from a normative rule about who is allowed to act, who is forbidden to act, who owns the object, who has access, or whose approval has binding authority.
- A role change alone is not sufficient; the rewritten story must materially change what is allowed, forbidden, required, or restricted.
- The rewritten policy must directly exclude, override, or contradict the permission implied by the source story.
- The rewritten story must fail the coexistence test: if the source story and rewritten story could both be implemented together as role variants, approval workflows, optional restrictions, broader/narrower access levels, or sequential process steps, the rewrite is invalid.
- Avoid dependency-like formulations such as "only after approval", "requires approval before", "once approved", "after authorization", or "after review" unless the rewritten rule directly removes the original actor's permission.
- Prefer strong policy formulations such as "must not be allowed to", "is prohibited from", "only [different role] may", "access must be denied to [original actor]", or "ownership must remain with [different role]".
- Do not rely on a changed runtime state alone.
- Do not generate a near-duplicate, paraphrase, prerequisite/setup story, or simple approval dependency.
- Keep the rewritten story realistic and plausible in a real backlog.
- The rewritten story should overlap strongly with the source story in feature intent, but the changed policy must make both stories unable to coexist as stated.

Internal validity check before finalizing each item:
1. Identify the actor, action, and object in the source story.
2. Identify the permission, ownership, approval, or access rule in the rewritten story.
3. Ask whether both stories could be implemented together without contradiction.
4. If yes, revise the rewritten story until the rewritten policy directly excludes or overrides the source permission.
5. If the rewritten story only adds approval, review, setup, or a stricter workflow, revise it.

Example:
Input stories:
[
  {
    "story_id": "1",
    "source_story": "As a support agent, I want to edit customer billing details so that I can resolve billing issues."
  }
]

Example output:
{
  "items": [
    {
      "story_id": "1",
      "source_story": "As a support agent, I want to edit customer billing details so that I can resolve billing issues.",
      "generated_story": "As a compliance manager, I want support agents to be prohibited from editing customer billing details because only finance managers may change billing records.",
      "transformation": "POLICY_TRANSFORMATION",
      "rationale": "The generated story keeps the same billing-details feature area but changes the governing permission rule by denying the source actor's editing permission and assigning it exclusively to finance managers."
    }
  ]
}
